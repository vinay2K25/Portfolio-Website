About Me

I am an undergraduate student at the International Institute of Information Technology, Hyderabad, with a strong passion for computer science and problem solving. My journey into programming began in 2023 when I attempted to build a 2D platformer game in Python — it did not quite work out, but something about that experience stuck with me: the idea that you could sit down, write some code, and have something real come out the other end. That feeling is what drove me to keep going. I learn best by building things, which is why my growth has been shaped less by textbooks and more by the projects I have thrown myself into — from a command-line music player to a strategy board game to a full-stack task manager. These days I find myself increasingly drawn to the user-facing side of software, to the craft of building interfaces that feel good to use. Outside of coding, I enjoy table tennis, tennis, and reading. As for where I am headed — I am still figuring that out, and I think that is perfectly fine.

 The Beginning
 Over the past year, I built a strong foundation in programming by learning C and C++, covering core concepts from the fundamentals of programming to working with arrays, linked lists, and dynamic programming.

 Building Deeper Foundations
 Building on that foundation this year, I explored several advanced data structures, including stacks, queues, binary trees, binary search trees, heaps, and hash tables, deepening my understanding of efficient algorithm design and problem solving.

 Alongside data structures, I also expanded my programming toolkit by learning Shell Scripting and Python. Currently, I am further broadening my skill set by working with HTML, CSS, and JavaScript to develop modern web interfaces.
